# Coverage goal

Behavioural end-to-end validation, **through the public API**, that `POST /move-a-piece` on `fisher-server` promotes a White pawn to a Queen when it reaches the 8th rank. Promotion rides the **existing** F0002 move contract: the HTTP status is still the verdict, and on success the response carries the board after the move â€” now with a `"Q"` on the destination instead of the pawn. Each scenario seeds a **known position** with a White pawn on rank 7 through the private `POST /private/setup-board` seam (F0002 rules **T-1**â€“**T-7**), so no test hand-plays a game to reach the last rank. The tests drive the running server over HTTP only â€” no internal calls, no server-state inspection.

What is asserted (black-box, no internal-state inspection):

- a White pawn pushed onto an empty rank-8 square returns `200` with `piece == "P"`, `capture == null`, and the destination cell `"Q"` in the returned board â€” proves **B-2** (promotion triggers on a White pawn reaching rank 8), **B-3** (it becomes a Queen), and **B-5** (the response echoes the mover `"P"`, the Queen shows only in the board);
- a White pawn that captures a Black piece on rank 8 returns `200` with `capture` set to the taken piece and the destination cell `"Q"` â€” proves **B-4** (promotion applies to the capture shape too) and **B-5** (the capture is still reported alongside the promotion);
- an illegal would-be promotion â€” a pawn push onto an **occupied** rank-8 square â€” is rejected `422 "illegal shape"` and promotes nothing â€” proves **B-4** (promotion fires only after the move is fully legal), so a blocked pawn is not silently queened;
- a non-promoting White pawn move keeps `"P"` on its destination, never a Queen â€” already proven by [TC-IT-F0002-004](../F0002-move-a-piece/IT-F0002.md#tc-it-f0002-004--a-valid-pawn-push-updates-the-returned-board) (a `d2`â†’`d4` push echoes `"P"` on `d4`), kept here for traceability â€” proves **B-6** (promotion never fires off rank 8).

The rules referenced above are defined in [F0004.md](F0004.md#rules); the seed contract is in [F0002 â†’ Test-support API](../F0002-move-a-piece/F0002.md#test-support-api--post-privatesetup-board).

# Test cases

Each case seeds one of the two [Seed positions](#seed-positions) first, then makes its move against `POST /move-a-piece`.

## TC-IT-F0004-001 â€” a promotion push replaces the pawn with a Queen

- **Given:** a game seeded to **Position #1** (promotion) via `seed(base, board_1())`, with the White pawn on `e7` and `e8` empty.
- **Input:** `POST /move-a-piece` with `{ uuid, from: "e7", to: "e8" }` â€” a straight pawn push onto the empty last-rank square.
- **When:** the client sends the request and deserializes the body into `MoveResponse`.
- **Then:**
  1. status is `200 OK` â€” proves the promotion push is a legal, applied move on the unchanged move contract (rules **B-1**, **B-2**);
  2. `piece == "P"` and `capture == null` â€” proves the response echoes the **mover** (the pawn), not the promoted piece, and a push takes nothing (rule **B-5**);
  3. in the returned `overview.board`, `cell(ov, "e7") == ""` and `cell(ov, "e8") == "Q"` â€” proves the pawn left `e7` and the destination now holds a White Queen, the substitution at the apply step (rules **B-2**, **B-3**);
  4. `cell(ov, "e8") != "P"` â€” proves the pawn was **replaced**, not merely moved to rank 8 (rule **B-3**).

## TC-IT-F0004-002 â€” a promotion capture reports the take and lands a Queen

- **Given:** a game seeded to **Position #1** (promotion), where the White pawn on `e7` can capture the Black rook on `d8`.
- **Input:** `POST /move-a-piece` with `{ uuid, from: "e7", to: "d8" }` â€” a forward-diagonal capture onto rank 8.
- **When:** the client sends the request and deserializes the body.
- **Then:**
  1. status is `200 OK` â€” proves the diagonal capture onto the last rank is legal and applied (rule **B-4**);
  2. `piece == "P"` and `capture == "r"` â€” proves the taken Black rook is still reported in `capture` alongside the promotion, and the echoed mover stays the pawn (rule **B-5**);
  3. in the returned board, `cell(ov, "e7") == ""` and `cell(ov, "d8") == "Q"` â€” proves the pawn captured onto `d8`, overwrote the rook, and was promoted to a White Queen in one move (rules **B-3**, **B-4**), so `"r"` is gone from the board.

## TC-IT-F0004-003 â€” an illegal would-be promotion promotes nothing

- **Given:** a game seeded to **Position #2** (blocked promotion), where a Black knight on `e8` sits directly in front of the White pawn on `e7`.
- **Input:** `POST /move-a-piece` with `{ uuid, from: "e7", to: "e8" }` â€” a straight push onto an **occupied** rank-8 square (a pawn cannot capture straight ahead).
- **When:** the client sends the request and deserializes the body into `IllegalBody`.
- **Then:**
  1. status is `422 Unprocessable Content` â€” proves the move is illegal, not malformed: promotion does not relax the pawn geometry (rule **B-4**);
  2. `reason == "illegal shape"` â€” proves the precise `IllegalReason` for a push into an occupied square (rule **B-4**, F0002 rule **B-12**);
  3. the `422` verdict itself proves nothing was applied â€” the blocked pawn is left on `e7`, never silently queened (rule **B-4**); the stored game is unchanged.
